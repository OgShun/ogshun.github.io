About Me

I am Yun Li, a postdoc fellow at CSIRO's Data61, aiming to design cutting-edge machine learning algorithms. Before that, I received my PhD degree in Computer Science from the University of New South Wales, Australia, under the supervision of Prof. Lina Yao and Prof. Wenjie Zhang. I received my M.S. and B.S. degrees from the Department of Computer Science and Engineering, Nanjing University, in 2019 and 2016, respectively.
I serve as the reviewer or program committee member for a set of prestigious conferences and journals such as MM, AAAI, CIKM, The Web Conf, TCSVT, KNOSYS, TMM, etc, and serve as the Sponsorship Chair for ADMA 2024 (Advanced Data Mining and Applications Conference). I am also a recipient of the 2021 Google PhD Fellowship (only 4 PhD student awardees in Australia) and the 2023 Dean’s Award for Outstanding PhD Theses (Top 10% of the PhD theses).
You may see my latest CV here (2024-01 edition).

Research Interest

My research interests lie in Data-efficient Computer Vision (such as zero-shot learning and meta-learning) and Transferable Machine Learning (such as domain adaptation and transfer learning), focusing on developing deep learning algorithms using fewer resources (such as human annotations) and having more applied domains.
My research is motivated by and contributes to various applications in computer vision, genetic analysis, medical image predictions, and healthcare informatics. Additionally, I have gained research experience working with other types of data, including spatial datasets, graph datasets, and short text corpora.
